SOME FACTS

GIRAFFE

Giraffes are the tallest mammal in the world.Giraffes don't need much sleep.Giraffes can stand half an hour after being born.

ZEBRA

The lion is the most prevalent predator of a zebra.Zebras use their stripes to camouflage themselves in tall grasses.White-colored stripes can be 18 degrees cooler than their dark counterparts.

PANDA

Pandas are the most expensive animals to be kept in zoos or captivity.A giant panda eats as much as 10kg of bamboo per day.Unlike most other bears, pandas don’t hibernate.

FOX

Foxes are great night-time predators.Foxes can make over 40 different sounds.Foxes only reproduce once a year.Foxes make use of the earth’s magnetic field to hunt.

ELEPHANT

They're the world's largest land animal.They communicate through vibrations.A baby elephant can stand within 20 minutes of birth.

TIGER

Tigers have soft toe pads which help them walk silently through their habitat.Tigers don't usually eat their prey at the kill site - but instead drag their prey into cover to feed.The vocal repertoire of tigers is vast - they grunt, growl, roar, moan, snarl, chuff, hiss and gasp.

Preserving Wildlife

PRESERVING WILDLIFE

Saving Habitat

This includes taking care of wetlands, forests, prairies, rivers and other places where wildlife thrives.Therefore, it is important to prevent water pollution as well as environmental pollution and avoid cutting trees.

Volunteer Works

The importance of volunteering lies in the fact that it allows people to stay in proximity to the wildlife and better understand them.Guide visitors about the wildlife at local animal sanctuaries or to help to rescue wild animals.

Resorting to Eco-friendly Products

Animals can consume the sprayed plants and polluted water.This will ultimately prove deadly to them when they enter the food cycle.Therefore it is important to resort to eco-friendly products that have no such adverse effects.

Giving water

Unlike humans, wild animals are not privileged with proper drinking water.Therefore it is a good service towards wildlife protection to provide water to these animals.

Feeding Animals and Birds

In certain instances, local wildlife doesn't have proper access to food and water, especially in certain seasons.Therefore, people can provide food and water to the birds and wild animals in their backyards.

Restoring Wildlife Habitat

humans must take care of all the other living organisms and create harmony between different habitats.Hence, people should contribute towards environmental protection by cleaning beaches, planting trees and banning poisonous agricultural chemicals.

Creating a Wildlife-friendly Home

Making homes wildlife-friendly is not complex work.Simple things like keeping the trash away from wild animals can contribute to this prospect.

Speak Up

Share your passion for wildlife conservation with your family. Tell your friends how they can help. Ask everyone you know to pledge to do what they can to stop wildlife trafficking.

Going to Wildlife Protection Forums

Another way of contributing to wildlife conservation is visiting national parks, zoos and aquariums.Several wildlife organizations and government bodies try to do conservation works by raising money through tourism.However, tourism can sometimes create adverse effects on wildlife.
